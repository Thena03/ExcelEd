<h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-center mb-4"> Standard Deviation</h2>

Excel has two functions to calculate the standard deviation of a dataset. The standard deviation measures how far a typical observation is from the average of the data. 
</div>

<div class="bg-white border-l-4 border-green-600 p-4 rounded-lg mb-6 shadow-sm"> 
<h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-center mb-4"> STDEV.P </h2>
This function calculates the standard deviation for the entire population. It is typed `=STDEV.P` <br>

  <img src="{{ '/static/STDEV.P.png' | relative_url }}" alt="Excel STDEV.P example"/> </p>
</div>

<div class="bg-white border-l-4 border-green-600 p-4 rounded-lg mb-6 shadow-sm"> 
<h2 class="text-2xl font-semibold text-center mb-4"> STDEV.S </h2>
This function calculates the standard deviation for the entire sample. It is typed `=STDEV.S` <br>
